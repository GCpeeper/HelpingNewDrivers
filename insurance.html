Insurance

Understanding the different types of car insurance is essential for every driver. Whether you're looking for basic coverage or more comprehensive protection, knowing what each type of insurance offers will help you make informed decisions.

This guide breaks down the most common types of auto insurance, including what they cover and whether they're required, so you can choose the right coverage for your needs and drive with peace of mind.

Insurance Type Description What It Covers Is It Required? Estimated Pricing (Monthly)
Liability Insurance Website with more details Covers costs if you are at fault in an accident and cause injury or property damage to others. It's the most basic form of auto insurance. - Bodily injury to other people
- Property damage to other vehicles or property		 Yes, in most states (coverage minimums vary) $50 - $100, depending on coverage limits
Personal Injury Protection (PIP) Website with more details Covers medical expenses and sometimes lost wages for you and your passengers, regardless of fault in an accident. - Medical bills
- Lost wages
- Rehabilitation
- Sometimes funeral expenses		 Required in "no-fault" states; optional elsewhere $10 - $50, depending on state and coverage
Collision Insurance Video with more details Covers damage to your own vehicle resulting from a collision with another vehicle or object, regardless of fault. - Repairs to your car after a collision
- Replacement if the car is totaled		 Optional, but may be required by lenders or leasing companies $30 - $100, depending on vehicle value
Uninsured Motorist Coverage Website with more details Protects you if you are hit by a driver who does not have insurance or has insufficient coverage to pay for damages. - Medical expenses
- Lost wages
- Property damage (in some cases)		 Required in some states; optional in others $10 - $50, depending on coverage
Comprehensive Coverage Website with more details Covers damage to your car caused by non-collision events like theft, vandalism, natural disasters, or hitting an animal. - Theft
- Vandalism
- Fire, natural disasters
- Damage from falling objects, hitting animals		 Optional, but may be required by lenders or leasing companies $15 - $50, depending on vehicle value

Disclaimer: the table above does not present every type of insurance; it only contains the most common ones.
